online resources

A few online resources that you may find useful:

  • PSU Sage Notebook server (open source alternative to Maple, Mathematica, and Matlab; runs in a web browser)
  • Lurch (open source mathematics validation software)
  • Group Explorer (free visualization software for abstract algebra)
  • Whitman Calculus (free calculus text)
  • One-Variable Calculus Resources (lots of resources for the first two semesters of calculus; hosted by the MAA Mathematical Sciences Digital Library)
  • Abstract Algebra: Theory and Applications (open source abstract algebra textbook)
  • A First Course in Linear Algebra (open source linear algebra textbook)
  • Interactive Real Analysis (free online real analysis textbook)
  • Wolfram|Alpha (a computational knowledge engine)
  • MIT Mathematics OpenCourseWare (free course material and videos for mathematics courses, offered through the MIT OpenCourseWare Project, also available through iTunesU)
  • Ever wonder, "When will I use math?" If so, check out http://www.whenwilliusemath.com/.
  • Visual Calculus (a cool website with Flash tutorials covering a variety of topics in calculus)
  • Paul's Online Math Notes (an excellent website by Paul Dawkins at Lamar University)
  • trig unit circle (PDF file)

applets

Here are some applets, mostly illustrating various concepts from calculus:

  • Calculus Applets (a comprehensive collection of excellent calculus applets)
  • Mathematics Visualization Toolkit (this thing won't do your laundry, but it does almost everything else)
  • Java applets by Deej Heath (lots of Java applets, including some calculus applets)
  • GeoGebra applets by Elisha Peterson (small collection of GeoGebra applets; good for Calculus III stuff)
  • Blaise applets by Elisha Peterson (more applets; good for Calculus III stuff)
