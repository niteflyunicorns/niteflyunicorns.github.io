HOMEWORK EXPECTATIONS

  • The most important thing:  DO YOUR HOMEWORK!!!
  • Late homework will not be accepted without prior approval from me.
  • Each homework assignment will consist of odd and even numbered problems from the textbook.
  • Odd numbered problems will be graded for completeness.
  • Two of the even numbered problems will be graded for correctness.
  • Show all of your work (especially on the evens).
  • Circle, box, or highlight your final answer.
  • Be neat and organized (both of us should be able to read what you wrote).
  • Put the homework number and section number(s) somewhere on the first page.
  • Do the even-numbered problems for each section on a separate sheet of paper (or at least separate them from the odds).
  • Please do not fold the corners of your homework.  Use a staple!
  • It is your responsibility to check the answers to the odd-numbered problems.
  • I recommend doing your homework in pencil (if you aren’t making mistakes, then you aren’t learning).
  • If homework is not taken seriously, then it will be very difficult to do well in this class.
  • Be aware of the fact that an excellent homework grade can raise your overall class average by a letter grade and that a poor homework grade can lower your overall class average by a letter grade.
  • I am here to help you.  If you are having difficulty with the material and/or your homework, then please ask for help.  I have regular office hours.  If these hours don’t work for you, then we can schedule a separate time to meet.
  • Class will usually begin with an opportunity to ask questions about homework.  However,if you have more than a couple of questions, then we should meet to discuss your questions.
  • You are responsible for writing up and turning in your own homework, but I recommend doing your homework in groups as often as you can.
  • If you need help, then ask!
